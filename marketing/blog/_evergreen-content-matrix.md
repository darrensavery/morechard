# Morechard Blog — Evergreen & Seasonal Content Matrix

A 12-month editorial plan for the Morechard blog. Built for a **fortnightly** cadence
(26 slots/year), it covers the three core pillars — pocket money, child financial
literacy, and money in separated families — and anchors timely posts to the UK money
calendar (My Money Week, Talk Money Week, the new tax year, Black Friday, etc.).

> **Positioning reminder:** the lead hook is *real-chore-data financial education* —
> kids learning money through actual earning and saving. The separated-family angle is a
> supporting pillar, never the headline. Keep school references collaborative (blame the
> crowded syllabus and timing, not teachers). Use en dashes (–), not em dashes. No
> unverifiable user-count claims.

---

## How to use this file

- **Calendar** = the locked fortnightly schedule with the recommended post for each slot.
- **Topic bank** = full briefs (keyword, intent, angle, format, internal links) for every
  planned post, grouped by pillar.
- **Swap-in bank** = evergreen extras with no fixed date — use them to fill a gap, replace
  a post that no longer fits, or get ahead of schedule.
- Update `dateModified` on cornerstone posts each year rather than rewriting them.

## Topic bank — full briefs

### Pillar A · Pocket money & rates

**A1. How Much Pocket Money Should I Give? UK Averages by Age (2026)** *(cornerstone)*
- **Keyword:** how much pocket money / average pocket money UK
- **Intent:** informational, very high volume; strong featured-snippet + AI-Overview target
- **Angle:** an age band table (5, 8, 11, 14, 16) with sensible ranges, then "the number matters less than the system around it" → earning, saving, goals.
- **Format:** pillar + data table; refresh `dateModified` yearly.
- **Internal links:** Pocket Money Index, A2 (chores link), A7 (start age).

**A2. Should Pocket Money Be Linked to Chores? The Honest Answer**
- **Keyword:** should pocket money be linked to chores / paying kids for chores
- **Intent:** decision-stage, divisive question = high engagement + comments/shares
- **Angle:** present both camps fairly, land on a hybrid (baseline allowance + earn-extra) — exactly what the product models. Cross-link the `effort-reward-connection` pillar rather than repeating it.
- **Format:** opinion-led explainer with a decision framework.

**A3. When Should You Start Giving Pocket Money? The Right Age to Begin**
- **Keyword:** what age to start pocket money / best age for pocket money
- **Intent:** informational; new-parent entry point into the topic
- **Angle:** readiness signals over a fixed age; what a 4-year-old vs 7-year-old can grasp.

**A4. Weekly vs Monthly Pocket Money: Which Builds Better Habits?**
- **Keyword:** weekly or monthly pocket money
- **Intent:** comparison; snippet-friendly
- **Angle:** cash-flow lesson — monthly teaches budgeting/delayed gratification; weekly suits younger kids. Tie to digital tracking.

**A5. Pocket Money Rates by Age: A Parent's Quick Cheat Sheet** *(snippet play)*
- **Keyword:** pocket money by age / pocket money chart
- **Intent:** quick-answer; built to win the featured snippet A1 doesn't
- **Angle:** scannable, printable one-screen reference; links up to A1 for the detail.

### Pillar B · Financial literacy by age

**B1. How to Teach a 5-Year-Old About Money (Without It Feeling Like a Lesson)**
- **Keyword:** teaching young children about money
- **Angle:** play-based, concrete coins, save/spend jars, "wait for it" games.

**B2. Teaching Tweens About Money: The 8–12 Window That Matters Most**
- **Keyword:** teaching tweens about money / financial literacy age 10
- **Angle:** the habit-forming window; introducing goals, comparison shopping, first independence.

**B3. Teaching Teenagers About Money Before They Leave Home**
- **Keyword:** teaching teenagers about money / financial literacy for teens
- **Angle:** subscriptions, first earnings, debit cards, the basics they'll need at 18.

**B4. The Money Conversations to Have at Every Age (A Parent's Roadmap)** *(pillar)*
- **Keyword:** talking to kids about money / age-by-age money guide
- **Angle:** single roadmap from 4 to 16, each stage links to a deeper post (hub-and-spoke).

**B5. Why Money Isn't Taught Much at School – and What to Do at Home**
- **Keyword:** financial education in schools UK
- **Angle:** *school-friendly framing* — the syllabus is full and time is tight, so home is where the habits stick. No teacher-blaming. Soft tie to the schools/Learning Lab offer.

### Pillar C · Chores, earning & responsibility

**C1. Age-Appropriate Chores: What Kids Can Actually Do at Each Age** *(high volume)*
- **Keyword:** age appropriate chores / chore list by age
- **Intent:** very high volume, strong Pinterest/save behaviour
- **Format:** age-banded checklist (printable); links to C4 rate guide.

**C2. How to Set Up a Chore Chart That Actually Sticks**
- **Keyword:** chore chart ideas / how to make a chore chart
- **Angle:** why paper charts fail; consistency, visibility, follow-through → the case for tracking.

**C3. Getting Kids to Do Chores Without the Nagging**
- **Keyword:** how to get kids to do chores
- **Intent:** pain-point query, huge volume
- **Angle:** ownership, natural consequences, the earn-link as motivation (not bribery).

**C4. What's a Fair Rate for Each Chore? A UK Pricing Guide**
- **Keyword:** how much to pay kids for chores
- **Angle:** task-by-task ranges; ties directly to the in-app **Rate Guide** and Pocket Money Index. A natural backlink magnet.

**C5. Paid vs Unpaid Chores: Where to Draw the Line at Home**
- **Keyword:** paying kids for chores / which chores should be paid
- **Angle:** "family contribution" jobs vs "extra earning" jobs — the model the app uses.

### Pillar D · Saving, goals & spending

**D1. How to Teach Kids to Save (When They Want to Spend It All)**

## SEO & GEO notes

- **Cornerstones get the internal-link love:** A1 (how much), B4 (age roadmap), C1 (chores
  by age) and C4 (fair rates) are hubs — point new posts at them and keep them updated.
- **Pocket Money Index synergy:** every rates/chores post (A1, A4, A5, C1, C4) should link
  to the Index and, once live, cite its data. That data is the moat and the backlink hook.
- **Snippet & AI-Overview targeting:** A1, A4, A5, C1 answer a clear question — open with a
  concise, liftable answer (40–60 words) before the long-form, plus a table where natural.
- **Schema:** keep the existing frontmatter pattern (`title`, `slug`, `type`,
  `description`, `author`, `datePublished`, `dateModified`, `schemaType`). Use
  `schemaType: WebPage` for guides; consider `FAQPage` blocks on the high-question posts
  (A1, A2, C3). Add `Article` schema for timely/seasonal posts.
- **Refresh, don't churn:** update `dateModified` and the year in titles on cornerstones
  each January/June rather than publishing near-duplicates.
- **Tone guardrails (recap):** lead with financial-education benefit, not the
  separated-family niche; en dashes not em dashes; no unverifiable user-count claims;
  collaborative on schools.
