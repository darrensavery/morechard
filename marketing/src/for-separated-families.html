Common questions

Everything separated parents ask first.

Does Morechard work if we don’t speak much?
Yes. Morechard is designed for low-communication co-parenting. Each household can independently log chores, approvals, and payments — the shared ledger is the source of truth without anyone having to coordinate by message.
Can both parents see and approve chores?
Yes. Both parents have full visibility into the same child profile. Each parent can set chores, approve completions, and pay out from their own device. Every action is attributed and sealed into the immutable ledger.
Is the audit log actually accepted by courts?
Morechard generates a PDF/A audit report with a cryptographic chain-head hash and a public verification URL. Each entry is timestamped and tamper-evident. Whether a specific court accepts it depends on the jurisdiction — but the format is built to meet standard evidence requirements.
What happens if one parent withdraws from the app?
The remaining parent and the child retain full access. The withdrawn parent’s historical ledger entries are preserved (and remain cryptographically sealed), but they no longer add new entries. The record they leave behind is still verifiable.
Can step-parents or grandparents be added?
Yes. Additional household members can be invited as Boost-only contributors — they can send rewards and view activity without becoming a primary approver. This keeps the parental Choice Architect role clean while allowing the wider family to participate.
Who owns the data?
Your family. Always. Morechard does not sell, monetise, or use the underlying data. Either parent can export the complete ledger at any time. The cryptographic chain remains verifiable independently, even outside the app.
