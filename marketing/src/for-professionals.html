Common questions

For practitioners.

Is the export admissible as evidence?
Morechard generates a PDF/A-2b export with embedded XMP metadata, a chain-head SHA-256 hash, and a public verification URL. The underlying ledger is append-only and cryptographically chained. Whether a specific court admits the export depends on jurisdictional rules of evidence — but the format is built to meet standard documentary evidence requirements.
What jurisdiction does Morechard’s data sit in?
Family data is stored on Cloudflare D1, with replicas confined to UK and EU regions. No data is routed to or stored in non-EEA jurisdictions. Compliance: UK GDPR, EU GDPR, COPPA, GDPR-K, and Polish NSFE alignment for financial education content.
How is the ledger’s tamper-evidence demonstrated?
Each ledger entry is hashed (SHA-256) together with the prior entry’s hash, producing a chain. A single altered byte in any entry — past or present — breaks every subsequent hash in the chain. The chain-head hash is embedded in every export and independently verifiable at a public URL.
Do both parents need accounts for the record to be useful?
No, but two-parent records are stronger. A single-parent ledger is still cryptographically sealed and exportable. A two-parent ledger additionally attributes each entry to the parent who recorded it, producing a richer evidentiary picture.
Is there a referral or commission scheme?
No commission. No paid referral scheme. Practitioners who join the partner waitlist receive a one-page client pack at launch and ongoing updates. Vouching is optional and only after a practitioner has used the product themselves.
Can a mediator be added as a neutral observer?
Not in the current scope — Morechard accounts are family-owned and not designed for third-party observer roles. A mediator can request that the family generate a PDF audit export at any review point, which can be independently verified without an account.
What if a client asks Morechard to delete their records?
The family controls deletion. A family-initiated account deletion (‘Uproot’) anonymises personal identifiers while preserving the cryptographically chained ledger entries — the audit chain remains verifiable, but no personal data is retained. Selective deletion of individual ledger entries is not possible by design — that’s the point.
