## 1. Scope and honest framing

This matrix covers the **launch scope** of the Learning Lab: 21 modules across 6 pillars, tiers Sapling (10–12), Oak (13–15) and Canopy (16+). The Sprout tier (6–9) is reserved for Phase 2 and is mapped here only at framework level, not module level.

**A note on framework fit, stated plainly:**

- The **PSHE programme of study** and the **money/financial strands of the three national curricula** (England, Wales, Scotland) are genuine financial-education frameworks. The Lab maps to these directly and strongly.
- The **Gatsby Benchmarks are a careers-guidance framework, not a financial-education one.** The Lab can legitimately claim to *contribute to* Benchmark 2 (labour market information) and Benchmark 4 (linking curriculum learning to careers) through its Earning & Value pillar. It cannot and should not claim full Gatsby alignment — the remaining six benchmarks concern careers programmes, employer/workplace encounters, FE/HE encounters and personal guidance, which the Lab does not provide. This document marks Gatsby as *contributory only* throughout.

## 4. Framework detail and statutory references

### 4.1 England — National Curriculum (statutory) ✓ verified

Financial education is statutory in England within **Citizenship** and **Mathematics** at Key Stages 3 and 4. Both quotations below are confirmed verbatim against the DfE programmes of study [S1].

**Citizenship KS3 (programme of study) ✓:** *"the functions and uses of money, the importance and practice of budgeting, and managing risk."*
→ Mapped by: Effort vs Reward, Needs vs Wants, Banking 101, Patience, Opportunity Cost.

**Citizenship KS4 (programme of study) ✓:** *"income and expenditure, credit and debt, insurance, savings and pensions, financial products and services, and how public money is raised and spent."*
→ Mapped by:
- *income and expenditure* → Effort vs Reward, Taxes & Net Pay, Needs vs Wants
- *credit and debt* → The Interest Trap, Good vs Bad Debt, Credit Scores
- *savings* → The Snowball, Banking 101, Compound Growth
- *how public money is raised and spent* → Taxes & Net Pay
- *insurance* → Insurance & Protection (M16) — **built** (closed G1)
- *pensions* → Pensions & The Long Game (M19) — **built** (closed G2)
- *financial products and services (breadth)* → partial only

**Mathematics KS3/KS4 (programme of study):** percentages, ratio and proportion, and financial mathematics including *"solve problems involving … simple interest … [and] compound interest."*
→ Mapped directly by every module with a real-data numeracy Lab: Taxes & Net Pay (percentage deductions), The Snowball (compound interest), The Interest Trap (debt growth), Compound Growth, Inflation (real-terms value), Banking 101.

### 4.2 PSHE Association Programme of Study (2026 edition) ✓ verified

Verified against the **PSHE Association Programme of Study for PSHE education, KS1–5, 2026 edition** (implementation from 1 September 2026, reflecting the DfE's July 2025 statutory RSHE guidance) [S2]. The 2026 edition supersedes the 2020 one and **no longer uses the old `L`-code system**; it is structured in two sections:

- **Section 1 — Relationships, Sex and Health Education (RSHE):** statutory. Notably, the **financial *harms*** (gambling, chance-based transactions in gaming/loot boxes, scams, fraud, money laundering) now sit here as **statutory Health Education**, not optional content.
- **Section 2 — Economic Wellbeing and Careers Education:** the personal/behavioural finance strand. Expected of all schools (and statutory for independent schools); the 2025 Curriculum & Assessment Review is also making Citizenship — which carries financial education — compulsory at primary. Learning opportunities (LOs) are numbered sequentially under each subheading, per key stage.

Launch tiers (11–16) map mainly to **KS3–KS4**; Canopy (16+) extends into **KS5**.

**KS3–4 Economic Wellbeing LOs → modules ✓** (verbatim, Section 2) [S2]:

| LO (KS · #) | Descriptor (verbatim) | Mapped by |
|---|---|---|
| KS3 · 1 | "different values and attitudes relating to finance, including debt; that families and individuals need to actively manage their finances" | Needs vs Wants, Opportunity Cost |
| KS3 · 2 | "how to manage emotions in relation to money" | Money & Mental Health |
| KS3 · 3 | "how to assess and manage risk in relation to financial decisions that young people might make" | Risk & Diversification, Good vs Bad Debt |
| KS3 · 4 | "how to manage influences on spending, including advertising, peers, climate and ethical business practices; what makes something 'good value for money'" | Advertising & Influence, Social Comparison, Needs vs Wants |
| KS4 · 6 | "about social and moral dilemmas relating to spending" | Giving & Charity, Social Comparison |
| KS4 · 9 | "how to budget effectively for different circumstances; the benefits of saving" | Banking 101, The Snowball, Patience |
| KS4 · 10 | "the opportunities, risks and challenges involved in different financial decisions, including investments, pensions, contracts, loans and insurance" | Compound Growth, The Interest Trap (loans) — **GAPS: pensions, insurance, contracts** (see §6, G1/G2) |
| KS4 · 11 | "factors to consider before taking on debt and ways of managing debt" | The Interest Trap, Good vs Bad Debt, Credit Scores |

**Statutory Section 1 (RSHE) financial-harm LOs → modules ✓** — these are *statutory* Health Education, so coverage gaps here matter more:

| LO (verbatim, abridged) | Mapped by |
|---|---|
| KS3 Online life: "how to recognise online scams to extort money or information … ways to protect personal information and finances online" | Scams & Digital Safety |
| KS3 Personal safety: "the risks associated with gambling, and that chance-based transactions in gaming can carry similar risks; strategies for managing … influences relating to gambling" | **GAP — no gambling module** (see §6, G3) |
| KS4 Online harms: "about thinking errors (e.g. 'gambler's fallacy') and product design features associated with gambling, including in online games; the risks and harms of gambling-related behaviours, including the accumulation of debt" | **GAP — no gambling/loot-box module** (see §6, G3) |
| KS4 Mental health: "potential long-term mental health harms from addictive behaviours such as gambling…" | Money & Mental Health (partial) |

**Careers & Gatsby (Section 2):** the PoS states that KS3–4 Careers Education "allows schools to fulfil the statutory requirement to deliver comprehensive Careers Education and meet the Gatsby benchmarks" [S2]. The Earning & Value pillar maps to KS3 Careers LO6 ("employment, self-employment, entrepreneurialism and voluntary work") and KS4 Careers LO14–15 ("the nature of the labour market … the gig economy"). See §4.5.

**KS5 (Canopy 16+):** the KS5 Economic Wellbeing LOs independently confirm the same gaps and extensions — LO3 "salary deductions, taxation, national insurance and the features of a payslip" (→ Taxes & Net Pay), LO4 "pension contributions" (G2), LO5 "financial contracts … renting" (contracts gap), LO6 "interest rates, credit scores and credit agreements" (→ Credit Scores), LO7 "consumer rights … resolve disputes" (G4), LO8 "cryptocurrencies, scams and gambling" (→ Digital vs Physical Currency / Scams).

### 4.3 Curriculum for Wales ✓ anchors verified

Organised by six Areas of Learning and Experience (AoLEs), the Four Purposes, and cross-curricular frameworks. Progress is described by **Progression Steps (PS) 1–5**, not key stages. Confirmed against Hwb (gov.wales) [S3]: financial literacy is explicitly anchored in the **Mathematics & Numeracy** AoLE statement of what matters dealing with **"The number system"** — which *"provides learners with a basis for … financial calculation and decision-making"* (worked contexts named include taxation incl. Welsh taxes, mortgage calculations, exchange rates and APRs) — and is **complemented in Health & Well-being**, where learning *"could explore financial literacy through risk and personal debt, and its consequences."*

| Wales element | Mapped by |
|---|---|
| **Maths & Numeracy AoLE** — "The number system" statement of what matters (financial calculation & decision-making) | All numeracy-Lab modules: Snowball, Compound Growth, Inflation, Taxes, Interest Trap, Banking |
| **Health & Well-being AoLE** — financial literacy through risk, personal debt and consequences | Money & Mental Health, Social Comparison, Patience, Interest Trap |
| **Digital Competence Framework** (cross-curricular) | Scams & Digital Safety, Digital vs Physical Currency |
| **Four Purposes — "ethical, informed citizens of Wales and the world"** | Giving & Charity, Taxes & Net Pay, Advertising & Influence |

Relevant Progression Steps for launch tiers: **PS3 (≈age 11), PS4 (≈age 14), PS5 (≈age 16).** Per-module PS assignment is in the crosswalk (§5).

### 4.4 Curriculum for Excellence (Scotland) ✓ verified

Financial education is a **"responsibility of all"**, anchored in the **Numeracy & Mathematics "Money" organiser** (outcome code `09`), with reinforcement in **Health & Wellbeing** and **Social Studies** (people, economy and business). CfE historically frames four organisers: Financial **Understanding, Competence, Responsibility, Enterprise**.

**Money Es & Os (Numeracy, organiser 09) ✓** — wording confirmed against the Education Scotland Numeracy & Mathematics Es & Os [S4]. The `a` outcomes are verified at each level (additional `b`/`c` outcomes exist at some levels):
- Second level **MNU 2-09a**: *"I can manage money, compare costs from different retailers, and determine what I can afford to buy."*
- Third level **MNU 3-09a**: *"I can budget effectively, making use of technology and other methods, to manage money and plan for future expenses."*
- Fourth level **MNU 4-09a**: *"I can source information on earnings and deductions and use it when making calculations to determine net income."* — **maps directly to Taxes & Net Pay** (gross/net, deductions).

| CfE organiser | Mapped by |
|---|---|
| Financial **Understanding** | Banking 101, Inflation, Taxes & Net Pay |
| Financial **Competence** | the numeracy Labs, Opportunity Cost, The Snowball |
| Financial **Responsibility** | Needs vs Wants, The Interest Trap, Giving & Charity |
| Financial **Enterprise** | Entrepreneurship, Gig vs Salary |

CfE levels for launch tiers: **Second / Third / Fourth level and the Senior Phase.**

The 2026 PSHE Association PoS itself states that KS3–4 Careers Education "allows schools to fulfil the statutory requirement to deliver comprehensive Careers Education and meet the Gatsby benchmarks" [S2] — so the contributory framing below uses an established, sourced link rather than a stretch.

| Benchmark | Status | Mapped by |
|---|---|---|
| 2 — Learning from labour market information | Contributory | Gig vs Salary, Entrepreneurship, Taxes & Net Pay |
| 4 — Linking curriculum learning to careers | Contributory | All Earning & Value pillar modules |
| 1, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8 | Not addressed | Out of product scope |

**External-claim wording (use verbatim):** *"The Earning & Value pillar contributes to Gatsby Benchmarks 2 and 4 by giving learners labour-market information (employed vs self-employed income, the gig economy, gross vs net pay) and linking curriculum concepts to the world of work."*
